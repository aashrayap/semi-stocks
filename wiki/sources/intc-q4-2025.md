# Intel -- Q4 2025 Earnings

Intel's quarter did not break the leading-edge bottleneck. It showed real product and foundry progress, but the numbers still describe a company in transition rather than a company relieving TSMC scarcity today.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Q4 2025 | FY2025 | Q1 2026 Guide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $13.7B | $52.9B | $11.7B-$12.7B |
| GAAP gross margin | 36.1% | 34.8% | 32.3% |
| Non-GAAP gross margin | 37.9% | 36.7% | 34.5% |
| GAAP EPS | -$0.12 | -$0.06 | -$0.21 |
| Non-GAAP EPS | $0.15 | $0.42 | $0.00 |
| Operating cash flow | $4.3B | $9.7B | — |
| Client Computing revenue | $8.2B | $32.2B | — |
| Data Center and AI revenue | $4.7B | $16.9B | — |
| Intel Foundry revenue | $4.5B | $17.8B | — |

Intel ended the year with $14.3B of cash and cash equivalents plus $23.2B of short-term investments.

## Guidance

- Q1 2026 revenue: $11.7B to $12.7B
- Q1 2026 GAAP EPS: -$0.21
- Q1 2026 non-GAAP EPS: $0.00
- Q1 2026 gross margin: 32.3% GAAP / 34.5% non-GAAP

## Key Management Quotes

- **CEO Lip-Bu Tan:** Intel's first products on Intel 18A are an important milestone and the company is working to grow supply to meet demand.
- **CFO David Zinsner:** available supply should be at its lowest level in Q1 before improving in Q2 and beyond.
- **Management:** demand across Intel's core markets remains healthy even as supply constraints persist.

## Thesis Signal

**[[concepts/n3-wafer-crunch]] (primary):** Intel is still an optionality story, not a current solution to the leading-edge wafer shortage. Q1 guidance stayed soft at $11.7B-$12.7B, and management said supply bottoms in Q1 before improving later. That means alternative capacity is not yet large enough to unwind the main constraint.

**[[concepts/foundry]] (secondary):** Intel Foundry generated $4.5B in Q4 revenue and the first 18A products launched. That matters because it gives the repo a real alternative-capacity datapoint, but it still sits beside TSMC rather than replacing it.
