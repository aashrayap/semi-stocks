# TSMC -- Q4 2025 Earnings

TSMC closed 2025 with another leading-edge print that still looks supply-constrained. Revenue, margins, 3nm mix, and capex all point to the same conclusion: the N3 bottleneck is active and the company is still racing demand.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Q4 2025 | FY2025 | Q1 2026 Guide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | NT$1.046T / $33.73B | $122.0B | $34.6B-$35.8B |
| Gross margin | 62.3% | 59.9% | 63%-65% |
| Operating margin | 54.0% | 50.8% | 54%-56% |
| Net profit margin | 48.3% | — | — |
| Diluted EPS | NT$19.50 / ADR $3.14 | NT$66.25 | — |
| 3nm mix | 28% of wafer revenue | 24% of wafer revenue | — |
| Advanced-node mix | 77% of wafer revenue | 74% of wafer revenue | — |
| Capex | $11.5B in Q4 | $40.9B in 2025 | $52B-$56B in 2026 |

HPC represented 55% of Q4 revenue and 58% of full-year 2025 revenue.

## Guidance

- Q1 2026 revenue: $34.6B to $35.8B
- Q1 2026 gross margin: 63% to 65%
- Q1 2026 operating margin: 54% to 56%
- 2026 capital budget: $52B to $56B
- 2026 revenue growth: close to 30% in U.S. dollar terms

## Key Management Quotes

- **CFO Wendell Huang:** strong demand for leading-edge process technologies supported Q4 and should continue into Q1.
- **CEO C.C. Wei:** recent AI demand remained very positive, with customers and cloud providers continuing to ask for more capacity.
- **CEO C.C. Wei:** TSMC's wafer supply remains the bottleneck, not power.

## Thesis Signal

**[[concepts/n3-wafer-crunch]] (primary):** This is the core manufacturing confirmation. Q4 revenue reached $33.73B, 3nm already represented 28% of wafer revenue, advanced nodes reached 77%, and Q1 guide stepped up again to $34.6B-$35.8B. If the constraint were easing, these prints would not look like this.

**[[concepts/foundry]] (secondary):** TSMC's $52B-$56B 2026 capital budget shows how much supply response is required just to stay near demand. Even with that spend, management still frames wafer supply as the active bottleneck.
