# Micron -- Q2 FY2026 Earnings

This was not a normal upcycle quarter. Micron posted record revenue, margins, EPS, and free cash flow, then guided sharply higher again. The numbers look like shortage economics, not a mild recovery.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Q2 FY2026 | Q1 FY2026 | Q2 FY2025 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $23.86B | $13.64B | $8.05B |
| GAAP gross margin | 74.4% | 56.0% | 36.8% |
| Non-GAAP gross margin | 74.9% | 56.8% | 37.9% |
| GAAP EPS | $12.07 | $4.60 | $1.41 |
| Non-GAAP EPS | $12.20 | $4.78 | $1.56 |
| Operating cash flow | $11.90B | $8.41B | $3.94B |
| Capex, net | $5.0B | — | — |
| Adjusted free cash flow | $6.9B | — | — |

Cloud Memory revenue reached $7.749B in the quarter. Micron ended Q2 with $16.7B of cash, marketable investments, and restricted cash.

## Guidance

- Q3 FY2026 revenue: $33.5B +/- $750M
- Q3 FY2026 gross margin: approximately 81%
- Q3 FY2026 operating expenses: approximately $1.60B GAAP / $1.40B non-GAAP
- Q3 FY2026 EPS: $18.90 +/- $0.40 GAAP / $19.15 +/- $0.40 non-GAAP

## Key Management Quotes

- **CEO Sanjay Mehrotra:** record Q2 results were driven by strong demand, tight industry supply, and Micron's execution.
- **CEO Sanjay Mehrotra:** memory has become a strategic asset for customers in the AI era.
- **Management:** Micron is investing in its global manufacturing footprint to support customer demand.

## Thesis Signal

**[[concepts/memory-supercycle]] (primary):** This is hard confirmation that memory remains the binding shortage. Revenue reached $23.86B, non-GAAP gross margin hit 74.9%, and the next-quarter guide moved to $33.5B +/- $750M at about 81% gross margin. That is what a shortage phase looks like when supply stays tight and AI demand keeps compounding.

Micron is the repo's cleanest company-level confirmation that the memory thesis is still active even while Baker worries about peak-cycle risk.
