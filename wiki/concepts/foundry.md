# Foundry

Foundry exposure in this repo is the manufacturing-adjacency layer around the core N3 thesis. It is not the same thing as [[concepts/n3-wafer-crunch]], which tracks the leading-edge shortage directly.

## Why It Matters

- TSMC dominates leading-edge capacity, so alternative manufacturing exposure can matter when customers look for second-source or specialty capacity
- TSEM is the specialty foundry name in the current ticker map
- INTC sits adjacent to this idea, but in the repo it is primarily tracked under [[concepts/n3-wafer-crunch]] because the trade is about leading-edge optionality
- Intel Foundry produced $4.5B of Q4 2025 revenue and launched first products on Intel 18A, which makes it a real optionality datapoint rather than a purely theoretical one
- TSMC's 2026 capex plan of $52B-$56B shows how much scale the dominant foundry still has to add just to keep up

## Current Read

This is a secondary concept, not a main cascade stage. It matters as a hedge or optionality layer around the dominant TSMC-centered thesis rather than as the central bottleneck itself. The current evidence still says TSMC is the bottleneck and Intel is the possible future relief valve.

## Source Positioning

- **Leopold:** holds TSEM and very large INTC calls
- **Baker:** no major foundry expression outside downstream semiconductor bottlenecks
- **SemiAnalysis:** reinforces that leading-edge scarcity remains concentrated at TSMC
