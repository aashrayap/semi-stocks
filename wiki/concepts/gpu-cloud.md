# GPU Cloud

GPU cloud is the deployment layer where upstream chip scarcity turns into contracted revenue, booked capacity, and visible customer urgency. It sits downstream of [[concepts/n3-wafer-crunch]], [[concepts/memory-supercycle]], [[concepts/pluggable-optics]], and [[concepts/power]].

## Why It Matters

- Revenue backlog reached $66.8B at CRWV as of December 31, 2025
- Capacity was effectively booked through Aug-Sep 2026
- CRWV exited 2025 with 850+ MW active and 3.1+ GW contracted
- 100K+ GPUs were deployed in one quarter
- Customer demand broadened beyond AI labs into enterprise and sovereign buyers

## Current Read

GPU cloud is not the main manufacturing bottleneck inside the cascade. It is the monetization layer. If GPU cloud operators stay sold out and keep signing long-duration contracts, the upstream shortages are real. If utilization or backlog cracks, the manufacturing thesis weakens quickly.

## Source Positioning

- **Leopold:** CRWV calls worth $1.2B, his largest position by far. He treats deployment capacity as the binding constraint.
- **Baker:** CRWV common worth $43M. He treats GPU cloud as downstream evidence, not the primary trade.
- **SemiAnalysis:** Rental rates, lead times, and sold-out capacity are direct confirmation that supply is still constrained.

## Earnings Confirmation

- **CRWV Q4 2025:** Revenue +168% YoY, backlog 4x'd, "unable to catch up with demand signals," and capacity sold through late 2026.
- **NVDA Q4 FY2026:** $95.2B in supply commitments and $500B revenue visibility show that cloud demand is still pulling hard on the supply chain.
